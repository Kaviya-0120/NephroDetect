What is CKD?

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a condition where the kidneys gradually lose their ability to filter waste and excess fluids. It often develops silently and can lead to kidney failure if not treated early.

Your kidneys play a vital role in removing toxins, balancing fluids, and regulating blood pressure. When kidney function declines, harmful substances build up in the body — often without obvious symptoms until significant damage has occurred. Early screening helps protect your long-term health.

Prevention

Protect your kidneys with these daily health practices

  • Drink enough water daily
  • Control blood sugar levels
  • Control blood pressure
  • Reduce salt intake
  • Avoid smoking and alcohol
  • Avoid overusing painkillers
  • Regular health checkups
